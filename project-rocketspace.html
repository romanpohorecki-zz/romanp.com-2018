RocketSpace Apps

RocketSpace is a coworking space aimed at accelerating tech startups worldwide. In November 2016, RocketSpace asked Luxoft to develop a MVP app for its new campus in London opening in April 2017. In the span of five months the consulting team built and tested an iOS / Android app that allowed RocketSpace memebers access to the facility, room bookings, event viewings, and inviting vistors.

  • Client RocketSpace.com
  • Team size12
  • Design team size2 designers
  • Rolelead designer
  • PlatformsiOS & Android
  • Duration5 months

Three day design sprint

We started the project with a compressed three day sprint to establish the MVP at RocketSpace headquarters in San Francisco. We interviewed RocketSpace stakeholders and members to establish priorities “Members should be able to enter the building with bluetooth, book meeting rooms, invite visitors, and register for events.”

By day two of the discovery sprint we had a clickable prototype for the RocketSpace team to use on their phones, and this helped everyone visualize the functional objectives. By the last day we had mapped the core of the app and both teams were aligned on the minimum viable product.

Early wireframing

Early wireframes explored a room calendar to book rooms, but it was deemed too complicated to build or use. The team decided to build a room booking ‘wizard’ based on a few key parameters which allowed a user to see all future rooms in order of availability and features.

Wireframes as Information Architecture

A hybrid of wireframes and information architecture helped the development start mocking out the layout of the app early on while understanding how everything worked together. By the tenth revision and fourth sprint we had finalized the MVP inofrmation architecture.

User research

We tested a clickable Proto.io prototype with 10 RocketSpace members. The testing exposed a strong preference for exposed navigation and a wish list of things beyond our scope, which we captured for the client’s backlog for future versions.

Details & interactions

In the initial Book a Room design, each available time slot would be a discreet room result. When connected to the API, we found that one room with varying times could repeat itself closely together. This could quickly lead to a confusion. A prototype of a consolidated time picker helped demonstrated a consolidated solution.

Onboarding Animations

A simple series of animations helped visitors understand how to enter the building using Bluetooth, books rooms, get notifications, and invite visitors.
